Methodology

Four stages.
Full accountability.

Every engagement follows a structured methodology — from initial diagnostic through ongoing oversight. No stage begins without completion of the prior. No recommendation is made without independent verification.

01
Diagnostic
  • Financial and operational review
  • On-site assessment
  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Market and competitive analysis

A structured, independent analysis of the asset — financial performance, operational systems, service delivery, team structure, and competitive positioning. The diagnostic produces a clear evidence base before any recommendations are made. Duration and depth are calibrated to the assignment type.

02
Engineering
  • Priority sequencing
  • Systems and structure design
  • Resource and budget planning
  • Milestone framework

Development of the structural and operational architecture required to close identified gaps. This phase produces defined deliverables — not strategic recommendations in isolation. All engineering work is reviewed with ownership before implementation commences.

03
Implementation
  • Phased rollout oversight
  • Team integration
  • Vendor and supplier coordination
  • Progress reporting to ownership

Supervised execution of the engineered plan — with direct accountability to the ownership structure at each milestone. Implementation is managed to protect timeline, budget integrity, and quality standards. Reporting cadence is established at the outset of each phase.

04
Oversight
  • Ongoing performance monitoring
  • KPI and management review
  • Quarterly reporting
  • Strategic adjustment

Structured post-implementation oversight — ensuring that improvements are sustained, not temporary. For ongoing owner representation mandates, this phase extends indefinitely. For project-based engagements, a defined handover and stabilisation period is built into the scope.

"Engagements are accepted on the basis of genuine alignment — between the nature of the asset, the objectives of ownership, and the scope of what can realistically be achieved. This methodology is not adapted to justify a pre-determined conclusion."

Professional Profile

Mark Collins

Luxury Hospitality Asset Advisor · Founder, Lovely Anytime

Mark Collins is an independent luxury hospitality advisor with over two decades of executive leadership across independent hotels, branded collections, and investment-grade hospitality assets. His practice is built on a single discipline: structured performance improvement and owner-side representation, conducted with full accountability to the ownership layer.

His operational background includes senior leadership roles at The Standard, High Line in New York City and Island Outpost Hotels, as well as executive assignments across Europe, the United States, and the Caribbean. These engagements provided sustained exposure to complex ownership structures, multicultural management environments, and assets operating at the upper tier of the international luxury market.

His advisory work is distinguished by financial literacy and operational precision. He approaches hospitality assets from the perspective of the investor and owner — with rigorous attention to P&L architecture, cost control, CAPEX governance, and management accountability. He does not operate as a general consultant. He works as an independent principal, engaged directly by ownership to represent their interests.

Collins has extensive experience managing the expectations and operational sensitivities associated with ultra-high-net-worth ownership — including family offices, private estate principals, and institutional investors with luxury hospitality holdings. His operating style is discreet, structured, and defined by clear deliverables.

As founder of Lovely Anytime, he maintains a deliberately limited portfolio of active engagements — ensuring that each assignment receives the sustained attention that complex assets and their owners require.

Mark Collins accepts a limited number of engagements per year. An initial confidential conversation is the standard first step for all prospective assignments.
